## References used

### 1. https://www.blindbarber.com
- Hero composition: Full-width image-text overlay with aspirational lifestyle photography, centered CTA. "Your New Regimen" banner on clean backgrounds.
- Section order: "Who we are" > "Shop" > "Visit" > "Watch/Learn" — brand story before commerce, locations, then educational content. Trust-first sequencing.
- Copy voice: Conversational yet refined. Short, punchy sentences. Founders note is intimate ("there I am") not salesy. "Stay Handsome" captures casual confidence without trying too hard.
- Photo treatment: Product-centric with neutral backgrounds, lifestyle shots of actual shop spaces. Bright, clean lighting. Diverse customers and barbers in action shots.
- Trust signals: Named founder (Jeff Laub), 13+ year history, press from Vanity Fair and NYT, 5 physical cities, 2% charity pledge. Credibility through specificity.
- Conversion path: "Shop" and "Visit" in nav. Location pages per city. Product purchase is primary CTA.
- **Apply to this build:** (1) Named founder/owner voice in About, (2) "Stay sharp" type casual-confidence tagline energy, (3) Real location specificity (Franklin Ave, Waco), (4) Press/review specificity over generic claims, (5) Shop atmosphere photography style

### 2. https://www.brutalistwebsites.com (non-competitor — design reference)
- Hero composition: Grid-based, no hero per se. Content IS the layout. Raw structural presentation.
- Section order: Flat, no hierarchy pretense. Grid of entries.
- Copy voice: Minimal, utilitarian. Labels, not sentences. Function over flourish.
- Photo treatment: Monochromatic thumbnails, no decoration.
- Trust signals: None needed — the archive IS the authority.
- Conversion path: Direct click-through. No intermediary. No friction.
- **Apply to this build:** (1) Zero border radius everywhere (the brutalist DNA), (2) Thick solid borders instead of subtle hairlines, (3) Hard offset shadows (no blur, no glow), (4) Sans-serif geometric type (Space Grotesk), (5) High contrast cream + ink palette with bold red punch

### Cross-reference design thesis
Lead with Blind Barber's casual-confidence voice and location-specific grounding, rendered through brutalist visual language (hard edges, thick borders, offset shadows, zero radius). The result should feel like a street-culture barbershop zine, not a luxury salon site. Copy stays punchy and short. Every CTA goes to phone or walk-in. The neo-brutalist aesthetic signals "we're real, we're loud, we're good at fades" without needing to say it.
